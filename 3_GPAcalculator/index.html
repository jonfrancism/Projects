How to calculate your GPA

Grade Point Scale

A = 4.00 grade points

A- = 3.70 grade points

B+ = 3.33 grade points

B = 3.00 grade points

B- = 2.70 grade points

C+ = 2.30 grade points

C = 2.00 grade points

C- = 1.70 grade points

D+ = 1.30 grade points

D = 1.00 grade points

D- = 0.70 grade points

F = 0.00 grade points

- Note that "Pass" and "No Pass" grades do not count towards your GPA


To calculate your term GPA you need to divide your total grade points earned by the number of total credit hours attempted (also known as "units").

total grade points earned / total credit hours attempted = GPA



Total grade points earned can be calculated by multiplying the grade point (see grade point scale on the left) for your course by your credit hours attempted for that course.



Example:

You took a Physiology course that was 4 credit hours (or units) and a English course that was 3 credit hours. Your recieved a grade of an A- and a B+, respectively. You calculate your grade points earned for those courses by multiplying(*) your credit hours attempted by your grade points.

Physiology: 4 credit hours attempted * 3.70 grade points = 14.8 grade points earned

English: 3 credit hours attempted * 3.33 grade points = 9.99 grade points earned

Totals:

24.79 total grade points earned
7 total credit hours attempted



To get your GPA for those courses:
take your 24.79 total grade points earned and divide it by your 7 total credit hours attempted . Which results in a 3.54 GPA

24.79 total grade points earned / 7 total credit hours attempted = 3.54 GPA



If you don't want to calculate your GPA this way, that's okay! That's what the GPA calculator is for!
